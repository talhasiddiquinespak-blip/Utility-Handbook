Water Supply

Distribution, treatment & storage design
01
Recommended Standards for Water Works (Ten States Standards)
GLUMRB — Great Lakes Upper Mississippi River Board, Latest Ed.
The definitive regulatory-grade design standard in North America. Covers design criteria for intakes, treatment, storage, distribution mains, pumping stations, and pressure zones. Memorise minimum pipe velocity (0.6 m/s), residual pressure (14 psi/1.0 bar at any point), and storage requirements.
Gold StandardDistributionStoragePumping
02
AWWA Manuals of Water Supply Practices (M-Series)
American Water Works Association — Key volumes: M1, M11, M23, M31, M32, M36
M1 — Rates, fees, demand analysis. M11 — Steel pipe. M23 — PVC pipe. M31 — Distribution for fire protection. M32 — Computer modelling (EPANET basis). M36 — Water audits and loss control. Each manual is a standalone design guide for that pipe material or topic.
Pipe DesignModellingNRW
03
WHO Guidelines for Drinking-Water Quality (4th Ed. + Updates)
World Health Organization, 2017 (+ 2022 Addendum)
Global benchmark for water quality standards — residual chlorine, turbidity, microbiological limits, chemical contaminants. Underpins nearly every national standard in developing countries. Essential for justifying treatment train selection and quality targets in any design report.
InternationalWater QualityTreatment
04
Twort's Water Supply (7th Edition)
Twort, Ratnayaka & Brandt — IWA Publishing
The most comprehensive single-volume textbook for water supply engineers. Covers hydrology, demand forecasting, source works, treatment, distribution, hydraulics, and asset management. If only one book — this one. Widely cited in design reports and specification preambles globally.
Core TextbookHydraulicsTreatment
05
BS EN 805:2000 — Water Supply Requirements
British Standards / European Standard
Requirements for systems and components outside buildings. Covers design pressure, velocity limits, surge analysis, pipe jointing, and testing. Widely used on UK and EU-funded projects across South Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. Defines Maximum Allowable Operating Pressure (MAOP) and test pressure protocols.
StandardPressure DesignTesting
06
CPHEEO Manual on Water Supply & Treatment
Central Public Health & Environmental Engineering Organisation, India (3rd Ed.)
The authoritative design manual for South Asia (Pakistan, India, Bangladesh context). Contains per capita demand norms, peak factor tables, storage design, fire demand, and trunk main sizing criteria. Most WASA/PHED projects reference this alongside WHO. Know the per capita demand ranges: 70–150 LPCD rural; 150–200 LPCD urban.

Sewerage & Wastewater

Collection, conveyance & treatment design
01
Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities (Ten States Standards)
GLUMRB — Great Lakes Upper Mississippi River Board, Latest Ed.
The sewerage equivalent of the water Ten States — the single most cited sewer design document in North America. Design criteria for gravity sewers (min. velocity 0.6 m/s, min. diameter 200mm), manholes (max. spacing 120m), lift stations, and force mains. Minimum depth of cover, pipe slope tables, and house connection rules are all here.
Gold StandardGravity SewerLift Stations
02
Wastewater Engineering: Treatment & Resource Recovery (5th Ed.)
Metcalf & Eddy / Tchobanoglous et al. — McGraw-Hill
The definitive wastewater engineering textbook globally. Flow estimation, peaking factors (Harmon, Babbitt, ten Broeck equations), hydraulics of sewers, treatment process design, biosolids, and energy recovery. Every wastewater engineer should know the Harmon formula for peak flow. Chapter 3 (quantity of wastewater) is the go-to for flow calculations.
Core TextbookPeaking FactorsTreatmentFlow Calcs
03
WEF MOP 8 — Design of Municipal Wastewater Treatment Plants (5th Ed.)
Water Environment Federation Manual of Practice No. 8
The comprehensive design manual for WWTP design — screens, grit chambers, primary treatment, biological treatment (activated sludge, SBR, MBR), nutrient removal, disinfection, and biosolids. Used for detailed design of all major treatment components. Companion to Metcalf & Eddy for plant-level work.
Treatment DesignWEFBiosolids
04
BS EN 752:2017 — Drain & Sewer Systems Outside Buildings
British / European Standard — Replaced earlier EN 752 parts
The European framework for gravity sewer design: hydraulic design criteria, design flood frequencies (Table 1 — from 1-in-1 year for residential to 1-in-10 year for city centres), pipe roughness, surcharge levels, and manhole design. Mandatory reference on UKAID, EU, and ADB-funded infrastructure projects.
StandardHydraulic DesignFlood Frequency
05
Sewers for Adoption — Guide 7th Edition
Water UK (formerly WRc / Sewers for Adoption Guide)
The standard adopted by all UK water companies for adoptable sewer design and construction. Sets criteria for pipe materials, bedding classes, surcharge test, CCTV inspection, hydraulic design flows, and manhole specs. Widely used internationally as a construction quality baseline for new sewer schemes.
Adoptable SewersUK StandardConstruction
06
CPHEEO Manual on Sewerage & Sewage Treatment (3rd Ed.)
Central Public Health & Environmental Engineering Organisation, India
South Asia's most used sewer design manual. Per capita sewage generation (80% of water supply), minimum sewer gradients, Manning's n values, DWF and PWF calculations, septic tank design, and WSP design. Standard reference on all ADB/World Bank funded sanitation projects in Pakistan. Know: DWF = PQ × 1.5 + I (dry weather flow formula).
South AsiaDWF FormulaManning's n

Storm Drainage

Urban hydrology, drain & culvert design
01
HEC-22 — Urban Drainage Design Manual (3rd Ed.)
FHWA / US Dept. of Transportation — Hydraulic Engineering Circular No. 22
The most widely used urban drainage design manual globally. Covers the entire urban storm drainage design process: hydrology (Rational Method, NRCS), inlet design, gutter flow, culverts, open channels, detention basins, and outlet structures. Every drain engineer must know the Rational Method: Q = CiA, and how to select C, derive i from IDF curves, and calculate time of concentration.
Core ManualRational MethodIDF CurvesCulverts
02
TR-55 — Urban Hydrology for Small Watersheds (2nd Ed.)
USDA — Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS)
The standard method for runoff volume and peak discharge from small urban catchments. Introduces the Curve Number (CN) method — probably the most universally used runoff estimation method in the world. Know how to look up CN from land use and hydrological soil group, compute S (retention), and derive Q (runoff depth). Paired with TR-20 for detailed routing.
Curve NumberRunoff VolumeSmall Catchments
03
HEC-HMS — Hydrologic Modeling System (User Manual + Technical Reference)
US Army Corps of Engineers — Hydrologic Engineering Centre
The industry-standard software and methodology reference for rainfall-runoff modelling of catchments. Used for basin-level storm modelling, sub-catchment routing, reservoir design, and flood frequency analysis. The technical reference manual defines the methods (SCS unit hydrograph, Muskingum routing, etc.) that underpin modern drainage design. Knowing this software is a key interview differentiator.
Software ReferenceHydrographRouting
04
ASCE/WEF MOP — Design of Urban Stormwater Controls (MOP 23)
ASCE / Water Environment Federation — Manual of Practice
Comprehensive guide for stormwater control measures: detention/retention basins, bioretention, permeable pavements, green roofs, constructed wetlands, and infiltration systems. Covers hydraulic design of each BMP/SuDS type, maintenance requirements, and sizing methods. Essential for projects requiring stormwater quality treatment alongside quantity management.
SuDS / BMPsDetention BasinsWater Quality
05
CIRIA C753 — The SuDS Manual (2015)
CIRIA — Construction Industry Research & Information Association (UK)
The UK and internationally adopted reference for Sustainable Drainage Systems design. Covers the SuDS management train, volume control, water quality treatment trains, and design of each SuDS component. Mandatory on UK-compliant and many ADB projects. Introduces the SuDS 4 pillars: Quantity, Quality, Biodiversity, and Amenity — a framework widely tested at interview.
SuDSUK StandardManagement Train
06
BS EN 752:2017 — Drain & Sewer Systems Outside Buildings
British / European Standard (shared with Sewerage column)
Governs hydraulic design of combined and storm sewers as well as gravity foul sewers. Table 1 defines design storm return periods by land use type. Also covers surcharge risk assessment, hydraulic gradient analysis, and the performance levels required for different types of development — fundamental to any gravity drainage network design in a UK/EU framework.

Demand Calculation — Key Methods, Parameters & References (Know These for the Interview)

The formulae and parameters interviewers expect you to recall or apply immediately — organised by discipline.

💧 Water Demand

Average Daily Demand (ADD) ADD = Per capita demand (LPCD) × Population Urban: 150–200 LPCD (CPHEEO) | WHO: 100 LPCD minimum
Maximum Daily Demand (MDD) MDD = 1.2 to 1.5 × ADD (peak day factor)
Maximum Hourly Demand (MHD) MHD = 1.5 × MDD = 2.25 × ADD (combined peak factor) Some codes use 1.8 × ADD for distribution sizing
Fire Demand (ISO 291 / NFPA) Q = 220√P(1 − 0.01√P) (US, P = population in thousands) Min residual pressure at hydrant: 10 psi (0.7 bar)
Storage Volume Equalising: 15–20% of MDD | Emergency: 25% of MDD Fire: per fire demand × duration (2–4 hrs)
Key Refs: Ten States § | AWWA M1 | CPHEEO Ch.3 | Twort Ch.7

🌀 Sewage Flow

Dry Weather Flow (DWF) DWF = PG + I + E where P = population, G = per capita flow, I = infiltration (m³/d), E = trade effluent (m³/d) Sewage generation: 70–80% of water supply (CPHEEO)
Peak Dry Weather Flow PDWF = 6 × DWF (BS EN 752 / Sewers for Adoption default)
Harmon's Peaking Factor (Metcalf & Eddy) PF = 1 + 14 / (4 + √P) where P = population in thousands For P = 10,000: PF ≈ 3.3 | For P = 100,000: PF ≈ 2.0
Manning's Equation (Gravity Sewers) Q = (1/n) × A × R^(2/3) × S^(1/2) n = 0.013 (concrete), 0.009 (PVC/uPVC)
Self-cleansing Velocity Min. 0.6 m/s at full-bore flow (Ten States / BS EN 752) Preferred 0.75–1.0 m/s to prevent H₂S generation
Key Refs: Metcalf & Eddy Ch.3 | CPHEEO Ch.4 | Ten States § | BS EN 752

⛈ Stormwater Runoff

Rational Method (for A ≤ 80 ha) Q = C · i · A / 360 (metric: Q in m³/s, A in ha, i in mm/hr) C = runoff coefficient (0.10 lawn → 0.95 paved) i = rainfall intensity from IDF curve at Tc (time of concentration)
Time of Concentration (Tc) Kirpich: Tc = 0.0195 × L^0.77 × S^(-0.385) (min) Or: Tc = entry time + travel time in sewer
NRCS Curve Number (TR-55, larger catchments) Q = (P − 0.2S)² / (P + 0.8S), S = 25400/CN − 254 CN varies: 39 (open land/HSG A) → 98 (paved/HSG D)
IDF Curve (Intensity-Duration-Frequency) Derived from local rain gauge records — must reference national or regional IDF data. For Pakistan, PMD data + Gumbel distribution for frequency analysis.
Design Return Periods (BS EN 752 Table 1) Residential: 1-in-2 yr | City centres: 1-in-10 yr Critical infrastructure: 1-in-100 yr (check for flooding)
Key Refs: HEC-22 Ch.3 | TR-55 | BS EN 752 Table 1 | HEC-HMS manual
